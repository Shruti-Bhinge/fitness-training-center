Frequently Asked Questions

I'm new to yoga. Which class should I start with?

We recommend starting with our Hatha Yoga or Beginner's Yoga classes. These foundational classes move at a slower pace with detailed instructions on alignment and breathing. Our instructors provide modifications for all levels, so you can progress at your own pace.

If you're completely new, consider booking a private introductory session to learn the basics before joining group classes.

What should I bring to my first yoga class?

For your first class, we recommend bringing:

  • Comfortable, stretchy clothing that allows movement
  • A water bottle
  • A small towel
  • An open mind!

We provide yoga mats and all necessary props for beginners. As you continue your practice, you may want to invest in your own mat for hygiene and comfort.

How often should I practice yoga to see results?

Consistency is key in yoga. For noticeable results:

  • 1-2 times per week: You'll begin to feel more relaxed and may notice slight improvements in flexibility
  • 3 times per week: Significant improvements in strength, flexibility, and stress levels
  • 4-5 times per week: Transformational changes in body composition, posture, and mental clarity

Even practicing just 10-15 minutes daily at home can complement your studio sessions and accelerate progress.

Is yoga suitable for people with injuries or medical conditions?

Yoga can be wonderfully therapeutic for many conditions when practiced appropriately. We've helped students with:

  • Back pain and spinal issues
  • Joint problems (knees, hips, shoulders)
  • Hypertension and heart conditions
  • Diabetes and metabolic disorders
  • Anxiety and depression

Important: Always inform your instructor about any injuries or medical conditions before class. For serious conditions, we recommend consulting with your doctor first and possibly starting with private sessions.

Can yoga help with weight loss?

Yes! While yoga may not burn as many calories as high-intensity workouts initially, it contributes to weight loss in several powerful ways:

  • Active styles like Power Yoga and Vinyasa Flow burn significant calories
  • Builds lean muscle mass which increases metabolism
  • Reduces cortisol levels (stress hormone linked to belly fat)
  • Improves mind-body connection leading to better food choices
  • Enhances digestion and detoxification

For best weight loss results, combine our Power Yoga classes with our nutrition guidance and maintain a consistent practice.
